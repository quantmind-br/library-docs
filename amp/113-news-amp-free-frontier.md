Every Amp user can now receive daily free credits to use the full Amp experience, including our frontier `smart` agent powered by Opus 4.5. No payment required, powered by ads—turn them off if you don't want free credits.

If you're a new user, just [sign up for Amp](http://ampcode.com/signup), and download the CLI or editor extension.

If you're an existing user, go to [user settings](https://ampcode.com/settings#amp-free) to enable ad-supported free credits.

## What You Get

The free credit grant replenishes hourly, giving you a total of $10 worth of credits per day or roughly $300 of credits per month. When you've used up free credits, you can either wait for the hourly reset or purchase paid credits.

Amp's `smart` mode is currently driven by Opus 4.5 (with GPT-5 and Gemini-3-powered subagents like the [oracle](https://ampcode.com/manual#oracle) + [librarian](https://ampcode.com/manual#librarian)). You can also use `rush` mode, which provides faster inference at a lower cost per token, currently driven by Haiku 4.5.

(Note: The Amp CLI execute mode (`amp -x`), other programmatic invocations of `amp`, and the Amp SDK consume paid credits only, not ad-supported free-tier credits, because we can't display ads in programmatic or non-interactive contexts.)

## Really?

Really! Like everything we do, ad-supported inference is an experiment. We can't promise we can do this forever, but we've already rolled it out to a sizable beta group thanks to our [ad partners](https://ampcode.com/free) and it has been well-received. You can [apply](https://ampcode.com/free/ad-partners/apply) to become an ad partner.

Ads are text-only and never influence Amp's responses. If you don't like ads, you can opt out and just pay the cost of inference.

We invite new users to check out [our manual](https://ampcode.com/manual), which contains good tips for using Amp efficiently.
